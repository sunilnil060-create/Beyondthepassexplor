The best time to visit Bhutan is during the spring months of March to May and the autumn months of September to November. During these windows the weather is at its most pleasant, with clear skies and moderate temperatures — ideal for sightseeing, hiking, and taking in the Himalayas without a cloud in the way.

March – May

Spring — Bloom & Birdsong

In spring, the valleys come to life with blooming flowers and trees, and birds chirping through forests thick with rhododendrons in bloom — arguably the best season of all for hiking and outdoor sightseeing.

1

Wildflowers and rhododendrons carpet the trails, making this a favourite window for hikers and trekkers.

2

The Paro Tshechu festival is usually held in March or April, filling the Rinpung Dzong courtyard with masked dances.

3

Days are mild and skies are largely clear, giving unobstructed views of the surrounding peaks.

Paro Tshechu — Mar / Apr
September – November

Autumn — Clear Skies & Colour

In the fall, the weather stays clear and sunny, and the landscapes are adorned with vibrant autumn colours. It's the other peak season, and for many the most reliable stretch of good weather all year.

1

The Thimphu Tshechu festival is usually held in September or October, offering visitors a chance to witness colourful masked dances and traditional ceremonies.

2

Mountain passes are open and roads are in good condition after the monsoon, making this a great time for road trips across districts.

3

Rice paddies turn gold across valleys like Punakha and Paro — a favourite season for photography.

Thimphu Tshechu — Sep / Oct

Good to know: Spring and autumn are peak tourist seasons, which also means higher prices and more crowds — so plan well in advance and book accommodations and tours early.

December – February

Winter — Quiet & Photogenic

For travellers seeking a less crowded and more affordable trip, the offseason from December to February is an excellent time to visit. The weather is colder, and certain mountain passes may close due to snow, but the payoff is a clear, refreshing atmosphere with far-reaching views across the Himalayas.

This season offers a fantastic opportunity for photographers — prayer flags swaying against picturesque snowy backdrops make for an idyllic, photogenic setting. Bear in mind that Bhutan's weather can vary considerably based on altitude, so pack layers regardless of when you travel. Bhutan welcomes visitors throughout the year.
