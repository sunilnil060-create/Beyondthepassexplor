The Royal Government of Bhutan introduced a set of incentives and policy measures to strengthen the tourism sector, effective from 1 September 2023 through 31 August 2027. The move reflects how important tourism is to Bhutan — creating jobs, bringing in foreign exchange, supporting related industries, and driving broader economic growth.

The Current Incentives

1

USD-paying tourists now receive a 50% discount on the standard SDF of $200, bringing the effective rate to $100 per person, per day.

2

The same 50% discount applies to the SDF rate for USD-paying children between the ages of 6 and 12 travelling as tourists.

3

Guests staying in Bhutan's border towns for up to 24 hours continue to enjoy a full SDF waiver.

A Brief History of the SDF

Bhutan has earned global recognition for its distinctive approach to tourism, deliberately prioritising cultural heritage and natural resources over sheer visitor numbers. Every guest now contributes a Sustainable Development Fee, a step up from the earlier $65 charge, in line with the country's "high value, low volume" tourism philosophy — welcoming fewer visitors, but ensuring every trip supports the Kingdom's long-term wellbeing.

Bhutan backs this philosophy with real action: responsible waste management, active efforts to cut carbon emissions, and a genuine commitment to eco-friendly travel across the country.

Recognised globally: In 2020, Bhutan received the Sustainable Tourism Award from the World Tourism Organization — a mark of its continued commitment to responsible, sustainable travel.

Where Your SDF Goes

The Sustainable Development Fee directly funds programmes that keep Bhutan Bhutan — preserving cultural traditions, protecting heritage sites and the environment, upgrading infrastructure, creating opportunities for young Bhutanese, and building long-term resilience across the Kingdom. Every night you stay is an investment in the place you came to see.
