The Department of Immigration, under the Ministry of Home and Cultural Affairs, is the sole authority responsible for issuing visas to Bhutan. Indian visitors are the exception — they travel on a permit rather than a visa. Every other nationality is required to obtain a visa before their trip.

Visa on Arrival

Nationals of Bangladesh and the Maldives are eligible to obtain their visa on arrival at the port of entry. Nationals of Switzerland and Thailand travelling on diplomatic or official passports are also eligible for a visa on arrival.

Even though visa-on-arrival is available, we recommend applying online ahead of time — it cuts down waiting time and makes clearance at the port of entry noticeably faster. Guests eligible for a visa on arrival will also need to pay the applicable SDF in USD at the point of entry.

Visa Fee & Required Documents

The visa fee is USD 40 per person and is non-refundable. Processing is usually quick, though it can take up to five working days, so it's best to apply a little ahead of your travel dates.

Getting to Bhutan

Bhutan is served by two national carriers, Drukair and Bhutan Airlines, both operating direct flights into Paro International Airport from destinations across the region:

Dhaka, Bangladesh Bagdogra, India Gauhati, India Kolkata, India New Delhi, India Kathmandu, Nepal Singapore Bangkok, Thailand

Travelling by road from India? Bhutan has four official land entry points:

SamtseWestern Bhutan
PhuentsholingWestern Bhutan
GelephuCentral Bhutan
Samdrup JongkharEastern Bhutan
